Recently graduated with a BSc in International Economics and Finance at Bocconi University, I have flanked my education with my interest for tech since I was 15, creating a small online community in 2011, that, albeit not managed by myself since 2014, still exists under a new name.

Having had a discrete success, with more than 90.000 registered users and more than 1.800 single visits per day, what started just as a hobby eventually spurred me to deepen my knowledge of programming languages, starting with HTML and moving on to Python and R. To complement my programming skills, I have become proficient in statistical software such as Stata and Tableau.

Before university, I completed my high school studies through the International Baccalaureate Diploma programme at Hockerill College, UK.

My thesis focused on the obsolence of 'paper currency' in favour of its digitalised alias, demonstrating that proactively reducing outstanding cash can increase overall welfare, government revenues and unlock crucial monetary policy instruments. I have conducted an empirical analysis on the case of the Swedish economy to show the veracity of my claims. Here you can read an extract of it.

Additionally, I occasionally write for The Market Mogul, an online think-tank journal.
